# Writing Guide — Notes on SEO & Growth

Reference doc for writing new articles for widiginanjar.com/notes/. Paste this into a new chat when starting a writing session, so Claude (or future-me) has the standards without re-explaining everything.

## 1. Structure
- 1 H1, a few H2s, rarely H3
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences), not long blocks
- One idea per paragraph
- Concrete numbers early, not saved for the end

## 2. Voice
- Vary sentence length deliberately — short, punchy sentences between longer ones
- Admit uncertainty out loud ("I don't know if...", "this is a guess")
- One hyper-specific detail per key paragraph, not vague phrasing
- No em dashes, ever
- Paraphrase sources fully, never lightly reword a quote (max one short quote per source, under 15 words)

## 3. Pre-publish checklist
- [ ] At least 1 cited stat/source with an outbound link
- [ ] At least 1 honest admission of a limitation or uncertainty
- [ ] Anonymised if referencing any client/third party (unless it's about myself)
- [ ] Tag matches one of the 6 official categories (see below)
- [ ] CTA at the end has both options: email + booking link
- [ ] Read it aloud once — flag anything that sounds stiff or unnatural

## 4. Internal linking (required, not optional)
- Minimum 1 internal link inside the body of the article (not just in "Related reading")
- Link should sit inside a sentence that's actually talking about that topic, not forced in
- Max 2-3 internal links per article total, to avoid feeling spammy
- Where it fits naturally:
  - Opening/context paragraph: if referencing a concept from another article, link it inline
  - Mid-article, when discussing a specific industry: link to that service page
  - Before the closing CTA: "Related reading" section, 1-2 relevant articles

## 5. Length and visuals
- 500-700 words is the sweet spot
- One visual per 400-500 words if there's real data to show, not forced

## 6. Titles that work
- Look for: a concrete number + a contradiction/tension in the same title
  (e.g. "I Rank #2 on Google... AI Still Doesn't Know I Exist")

## 7. The 6 official tags — don't invent new ones
- `ngo` (or `mission-driven` for broader framing)
- `hospitality`
- `b2b`
- `ai & tech`
- `ai search`
- `practice notes`
If unsure which fits, ask before writing it into frontmatter.

## 8. Pacing
- 1-2 solid articles a week beats 5 rushed ones
- Don't force publishing cadence

## 9. Before publishing, always ask:
"Who could be embarrassed by this, even if anonymised?"
If there's any doubt, generalise further or drop it. Case studies about real clients'
technical findings (canonical tags, noindex, etc.) are fine anonymised.
Stories about a real person's behavior, understanding, or communication (e.g. a client
going quiet, not knowing a term) are NOT fine, even anonymised, if traceable to one
identifiable relationship. Generalise into an observed pattern across multiple cases,
or don't publish.

## 10. Process
- Draft from real work/audits whenever possible, they're always stronger than
  abstract ideas
- Don't force an idea I'm not confident in, better to drop it than publish something
  half-convinced
